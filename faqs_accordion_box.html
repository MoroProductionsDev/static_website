Thorough removal of fleas from your home will take time and patience —
not to mention the help of a reliable product or two. The initial removal
of fleas from your precious pup shouldn't take long, but remember
that's just the first step in ridding your home of these unwanted irritants.
The following steps will guide you through the removal process.

  1. Comb your pet with a fine-tooth flea comb. Flea combs help remove
    flea dirt as well as adult fleas easily with their close-fitting tines.3 You
    want to immediately kill any fleas removed from your pup's fur by
    dunking them in hot, soapy water. If you try to crush or flush these
    sneaky insects, they might surprise you with their their jumping expertise
    —they're amazing escape artists.
  2. Bathe your pet with warm water and mild soap. Because your dog
    could be allergic to fleas (aka flea allergy dermatitis), you may want
    to consult your family vet before using a flea and tick shampoo
    —especially if your pup has raw areas or open wounds. A warm,
    soapy bath should instantly kill any missed fleas left after combing.
  3. Use a shampoo designed to soothe your pup's itchy skin if
    dermatitis isn't a concern. Adams Plus Flea & Tick
    Foaming Shampoo & Wash for Dogs & Puppies     will kill fleas on
    contact and offer relief with the addition of aloe vera and oatmeal.
    Adams Flea & Tick Cleansing Shampoo is also a great way to kill
    fleas. These products should only be used on a dog that's over 12
    weeks old. For younger pups, it's best to consult your veterinarian
    before using a flea and tick shampoo.
  4. A topical treatmentlike Adams Flea & Tick Spray is essential if you
    have an overwhelming flea infestation. This product should only be
    used on puppies 10 weeks and older. It's essential to follow the
    instructions listed on the flea and tick product and not apply more
    than instructed.
  5. Wash and clean your home to remove any remaining flea residue.
    Fleas need a host, but flea eggs, larvae and pupae can live almost
